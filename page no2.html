PURPOSE OF FIELD INSTRUMENTATION

The purposes of field instrumentation particular for application in construction works can be briefly described as follows:-

Control of Construction Safety:-

The response of instruments observed during each stage of excavation will provided to design engineers with valuable information in judging the safety of construction and any need for re-evaluation of design, modification of construction sequences and application of protective/precaution measures.

Verification of Design Assumptions:-

The actual behaviour of ground and retaining system recorded by the instruments during each stage of construction are very useful to ensure the actual behaviour and is within the disign limits.

Upgrade of Technical Know-how:-

The information collected from the instrumentation monitoring works could be used for the back–analysis of design and collection of software for improving the suitability and accuracy of the design method and the validity of the adopted design criteria.

Evidence to Damage Dispute:-

Frequently, the monitoring records which properly register the behaviour of the ground and surrounding properties throughout the entire construction period could be a powerful tool in the protecting the owner from dispute.

Key Objectives:-

Key objectives are to monitor wall deflections, movements of adjacent buildings, ground settlement outside the excavation during construction and monitoring of the GWT during construction.
